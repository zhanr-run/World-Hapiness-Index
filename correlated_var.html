The heatmap through presenting a matrix layout with color and shading to show the relationship between categories of values provides a better view of the correlation of the variables. As shown on the heatmap the relation between the score and economy has the highest correlation of 0.83, then goes to health with a correlation of 0.81 and the third most important variable goes to a family with a correlation of 0.80. Therefore, we can conclude for the World Happiness Report, their standard of defining happiness is based on economy, health, and family, which also applies the same as Maslow’s hierarchy of needs, the basic needs as always, the base of happiness. In another hand, generosity and corruptions seem to have less influence on the total happiness scores of countries.

Despite the lack of correlation of happiness score with the corruption from the heatmap, we cannot simply conclude the lack of influence on the happiness score by the corruption, the corruption level can have a direct linkage with countries economic development which is a key variable for the happiness score, therefore the data can have a survivorship bias in term of corruption. where the world happiness report hasn’t presented a more in-depth influence of this factor reflecting the happiness score.

Most of the corruption score lays not more than 0.4 scores, which based on the World Happiness Report it is the minor matter for scoring the happiness score. Whereas the family and economy overlap above score 1, reflecting the high proportion in defining the countries happiness score. As previously mentioned in the heatmap, the survivorship bias may lead the misleading importance of the corruption effect on the individual’s happiness in the real world, the importance of different variables can only be a reference of the real world but definitely lack to represent the real-world happiness.
